3. Market Highlights

  • EV adoption accelerating in Qatar: the light vehicle market is projected to reach ~89,981 units in 2026; public charging is set to expand from ~100 to ~1,200 stations by 2030, alongside a rule requiring new fleets to be at least 10% electric/hybrid from 2025.
  • Hongqi's Qatar debut: the Chinese luxury brand opened a dedicated showroom on Salwa Road via Pioneer Motors, following a quiet five-month soft launch — already reflected correctly in the Automania database.
  • BYD keeps expanding: the all-new 3-row Atto 8 has begun arriving across GCC showrooms, and BYD's Qatar 2026 lineup (Han, Atto 3, Seal, Seal 07, Shark 6, Song Plus) is largely listed as "coming soon" pending confirmed local pricing.
  • Premium Western/Korean EV wave incoming: BMW i3 (Neue Klasse, 800V), Mercedes C-Class Electric, Hyundai Palisade/Staria Electric, and the Tesla Model Y L (6-seat) are all slated for 2026 GCC arrival — none of these brands currently appear in the Automania database at all.
  • Saudi-built EV for the region: the Lucid Cosmos, produced in Saudi Arabia and targeted under $50,000, is being positioned as the most geographically relevant premium EV launch for Gulf buyers this year.
